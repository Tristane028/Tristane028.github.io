Who I Am

Hi — I'm Tristan Endo, a Math-CS student at UC San Diego (GPA: 3.52) graduating in 2027. I work at the intersection of mathematics and software — from algebraic geometry research to ML-driven trading systems and forensic audio analysis.

I'm currently an undergraduate researcher with Prof. Raymond Chou studying Springer fibers, and a Quantitative Research & AI Engineering intern at Tekly Studio in San Francisco, where I develop and backtest ML-driven trading strategies on financial time series.

My current research interest is NLP and AI hallucination detection. Outside of that I enjoy exploring the math behind the code — stochastic processes, algebraic geometry, and everything in between.

Neural Network Gaussian Process & Conjugate Kernels

Research with Prof. Alexander Cloninger at UCSD. Derived closed-form conjugate kernels for ReLU, erf, and tanh activation functions and studied how finite-width networks converge to the NNGP infinite-width limit.

Springer Fibers — Algebraic Geometry & Combinatorics

Ongoing research with Prof. Raymond Chou at UCSD. Built a Python tool to compute Schubert cells, valid Young tableau fillings, and Plücker coordinates for Springer fibers defined by Jordan block decompositions.

Brownian Motion & Stochastic Processes

Research with Prof. Robert Webber at UCSD. Investigated fractional Brownian motion peak distributions across varying Hurst parameters and derived conditional distributions for the Brownian Bridge and Ornstein-Uhlenbeck Bridge.

Audio Signal Processing — Demucs Source Separation

Used Meta Research's Demucs deep learning model to separate a raw audio file into individual stems (vocals, drums, bass, guitar) and analyzed each track's frequency content through spectrogram visualization.
